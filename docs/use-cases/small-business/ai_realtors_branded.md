# AI Video Maker for Realtors: Branded Tours in an Afternoon

**Short answer:** an AI video maker will not invent footage of a real house — and you should be suspicious of any tool that claims it can, because misrepresenting a property is a legal problem, not a creative one. What AI does solve for a real estate agent is everything *around* the walkthrough: the branded open and close, the neighborhood story, the narration, the market-update videos, the agent brand film, and the vertical and square cutdowns for every listing. Those are the pieces that take an afternoon per listing when you do them by hand, and they are the reason most agents post a raw phone walkthrough with no branding and no voiceover.

Shoot the property. Automate the production around it. With a pipeline that turns a written listing brief into a scripted, shot-planned, narrated, assembled video, a branded package per listing becomes a batch job rather than an afternoon — which is the only way it works when you are carrying eight to twenty active listings.

## What is legitimately AI-generatable in real estate video

| Asset | Generate with AI? | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Interior/exterior walkthrough of the actual property | **No — shoot it** | The footage must be the real home |
| Branded intro and outro | Yes | Consistent across every listing |
| Narration over your walkthrough | Yes | Script-driven text-to-speech |
| Neighborhood / lifestyle story | Yes | Conceptual, not property-specific |
| Market update / "just sold" clips | Yes | Information-led, high frequency |
| Agent brand film | Yes | Narrative piece; strongest AI fit |
| Buyer-education explainers | Yes | Evergreen, reusable |
| Vertical, square, and widescreen variants | Yes | One-click export |

The disclosure rule of thumb: anything that could be mistaken for a depiction of the property must be real footage of the property. Generated visuals belong in the branding, the story, and the education — and should read as such.

## Why agents stop making listing videos

The first three listings get the full treatment. Then the workload compounds:

1. Shoot the walkthrough — 45 minutes, fine.
2. Write the narration — 30 minutes, less fine.
3. Record voiceover, retake twice — 40 minutes.
4. Edit, brand, title cards, music — 90 minutes.
5. Export three aspect ratios — 30 minutes.
6. Repeat for the next listing on Thursday.

Steps 2 through 5 are three hours of mechanical work per listing that produces no differentiation. They are also, all four of them, automatable.

## An afternoon workflow for a week of listings

**Before you sit down:** shoot each property on a phone or gimbal. That footage is non-negotiable and it is yours.

**Hour 1.** Write a short brief per listing — the hook, three selling points, the neighborhood angle, the call to action.

**Hour 2.** Paste each brief in, set duration, and let the pipeline build the script, shot list, prompts, and narration. Review and fix the copy; this is the part only you can do.

**Hour 3.** Draft-render everything at the cheapest quality tier and watch it. Fix what is wrong and re-render only that.

**Hour 4.** Finish the keepers at high quality, cut your real walkthrough footage into the branded frame, and export the three aspect ratios for each listing.

One afternoon, a week of branded listing packages, and a consistent brand across all of them.

## Staying on the right side of accuracy

- **Never generate the property.** Real footage only for anything depicting the home.
- **Label conceptual visuals** as lifestyle or illustrative when there is any chance of confusion.
- **Do not generate "improvements."** Virtual staging is a separate, disclosed practice; generated renovations presented as current condition are a misrepresentation.
- **Keep the script factual.** Square footage, school districts, and HOA details belong to your MLS data, not to an AI expansion.
- A three-stage content scanner checks prompts, scripts, and finished output before download, but editorial accuracy is still yours to own.

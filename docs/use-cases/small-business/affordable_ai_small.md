## The cost most tools don't show you: labor

Subscription price is the visible cost. For a small business, labor is usually the bigger one. Consider what stands between a script and a finished video in a typical prompt-based workflow:

1. Break the script into scenes and shots by hand.
2. Write a prompt for each shot.
3. Generate a first frame for each shot, then re-prompt until it matches.
4. Keep the character in the same outfit across every shot.
5. Generate the video from the frame.
6. Stitch, add voice, sync, export in three aspect ratios.

That's six manual loops per shot. The ACT 3 AI product spec is direct about what happens at scale — a 40-minute film needs roughly 650 shots, and without automation directorial control collapses under deadline pressure. Your 90-second ad isn't 650 shots, but the same math punishes you: 25 shots × six manual steps is a full week you don't have.

## What automation removes, step by step

This is where an affordable tool earns its price. In ACT 3 AI, the steps above are generated rather than typed:

- **Script in, structure out.** Import a full script (Final Draft, PDF, plain text) or paste a paragraph, an article, a Wikipedia page, or raw text, and AI expansion builds it into beats, scenes, and shots. You set a target duration and the system shapes the story, scenes, and shots to fit it.
- **Automatic shot lists with cinematography attached.** The Beat → Scene → Shot planner auto-computes shot lists and embeds camera settings, lens choices, movement types, and framing.
- **Prompts written for you.** Shot-level prompt assembly combines the scripted action, visual instructions, and cinematography cues into detailed prompts; a "Mega Prompt" composer bundles narrative, style, camera, lighting, audio, and motion into one multi-modal prompt for the generative engine.
- **First frames and character sheets generated automatically**, with the correct outfits — wardrobe is managed per character with named variants like "Evening Party" or "Working in Office," so continuity isn't something you police by hand.
- **The right model picked per shot.** Multi-model routing selects among SDXL, Runway, Google Veo 3, ComfyUI, Hunyuan, and Wan 2.1 based on style and complexity, and auto-selection favors the cheapest engine that meets your quality constraints — a cost control you get for free.
- **Assembly handled.** Approved shots are stitched automatically with transitions and audio into a production-ready cut. Built-in text-to-speech generates the spoken lines from the script and embeds them into the timeline.
- **Export in the shapes you publish in** — 16:9 for YouTube, 9:16 for TikTok and Reels, 1:1 for Instagram — plus FDX, PDF, EDL, and MP4/MOV for handoff.

The differentiator isn't any single one of those. It's that they're chained. Automating one step and leaving five manual doesn't change your Tuesday.

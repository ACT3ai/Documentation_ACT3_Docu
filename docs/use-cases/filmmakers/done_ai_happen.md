# Done-for-You AI Movie Production: A Service That Makes Your Script Happen

A done-for-you AI movie production service is one where you hand over a script and creative direction, and someone else runs the production — the shot breakdown, the generation, the cast and set work, the assembly — while you stay the director through notes. You are buying execution, not software. The right service should take your script, come back with a watchable cut, take your feedback, and come back again with the changes made.

That is exactly what the ACT 3 **"Level 2 team"** package is: an optional service where our team takes your feedback into your movie and makes it happen. You can use us for part of your production team or all of it. You have a script; you want it made; you want to give all the notes you want and have them get accomplished. That is the arrangement.

This page explains how a done-for-you AI production actually runs, what you should expect to supply, and how to judge whether DIY software or a done-for-you team is right for you. It is not a price list — engagements are scoped to the production.

## Why indie filmmakers end up looking for this

The honest reason most indie AI films stall is labour, not talent. A 40-minute film needs roughly 650 shots. Each shot wants a prompt, a first frame, a consistency check against the cast and the set, a render, a review and often a re-render. The Wall Street Journal has described the work as "tedious" and the process as "madness," with scene consistency the major hurdle. There is a whole market of people who abandon projects once they see the hours involved — a single video can consume six weeks of manual effort.

You did not write a screenplay in order to become a prompt operator. A done-for-you service exists so that the person with the story does not have to become the person with the pipeline.

## How the ACT 3 Level 2 team works

The engagement is built around a loop, not a one-way handoff.

1. **You bring the script.** A finished screenplay is ideal. If you have a logline, a treatment, an article or a book instead, the platform's AI story expansion turns a high-level idea into a full screenplay with beats, scenes and dialogue, which you approve before anything is produced.
2. **We build the production.** The script is imported and auto-formatted, broken into beats, scenes and shots with cinematography metadata, and mass-automated: prompts assembled per shot, first frames generated, character sheets built with the correct outfits, sets sourced or built, shots rendered and assembled into scenes and episodes.
3. **You watch a real cut.** Not a mood board and not isolated clips — a full-length assembly you can watch end to end, including on a unified Adobe Premiere timeline where you can zoom around the whole runtime and see how the clips flow together.
4. **You give notes. All of them.** Story-level, scene-level, shot-level, performance, camera, look. This is the part of the deal that matters: you give all the feedback you want, and it gets accomplished.
5. **We rebuild.** Because the film is generated from structured project data, the entire movie can be rebuilt from the prior round's feedback — a production cadence measured in days, not weeks.
6. **You get deliverables.** 4K ProRes masters per shot, scene, episode or season; EDL, MP4/MOV and project exports; compatibility with Premiere Pro and DaVinci Resolve if you want to finish elsewhere.

You stay the director. We are the crew.

The package is deliberately divisible. Common shapes:

If your team is two people and a laptop, take the full production. If you already work in ACT 3 AI and just cannot get through act three before the festival deadline, take the surge crew.

## What you should supply

The engagements that go fastest arrive with:

- **A locked-ish script.** Rewrites during production cost more than rewrites before it.
- **Cast intent.** Age, look, wardrobe by situation — or trust our casting proposals and approve them.
- **A visual style decision.** Cinematic Realism, 3D Animated, Cartoon 2D or Anime, plus any reference images you want used as a mood board.
- **A target runtime.** Duration drives the pacing calculations that shape scenes and shots.
- **Your must-haves.** The three things that, if wrong, make the film not yours. Say them at the start.

## What the service is not

Being straight about scope saves everyone time:

- It is not a script doctor. We can expand a logline into a screenplay with AI assistance and your approval, but a done-for-you production assumes the story is yours.
- It is not distribution. We deliver masters in professional formats suited to festival submission and post-production; getting the film seen is your side.
- It is not a fixed-menu package. "Part or all of your team" means the scope is agreed per production.
- It is not a way to skip decisions. The films that come out well have a director who gives specific, opinionated notes.

## FAQ

**Can someone else make my AI movie for me?**
Yes. The ACT 3 "Level 2 team" is an optional package where our team takes your feedback into your movie and makes it happen, for part or all of the production.

**Do I need a finished screenplay to start?**
It helps, but no. AI story expansion builds a full screenplay with beats, scenes and dialogue from a high-level idea, and the platform can also ingest articles, books, Wikipedia pages or pasted text as a foundation. You approve the script before production.

As much as you want. The loop is built around your feedback, and the entire movie can be rebuilt from the prior round's notes — that daily-rebuild capability is what makes unlimited notes practical rather than ruinous.

4K ProRes masters at shot, scene, episode or season level, plus an export bridge covering FDX, PDF, EDL and MP4/MOV, and compatibility with Premiere Pro and DaVinci Resolve.

**Can I take over the production myself later?**
Yes — the production lives in an ACT 3 AI project your Organization owns, so you can continue in the platform with your own team whenever you want. That is a common path: we build the first full cut, you drive the refinement.

# Outsource Your AI Video Production to Our Level-2 Team

**Short answer:** you can outsource AI video production in three ways — hire a freelancer, retain a traditional agency, or use a done-for-you team that operates the AI production platform on your behalf. The third option is newest and, for most stretched marketing teams, the most practical: you keep the creative direction and the notes; someone else does the operating.

That is what the **ACT 3 "Level 2 team"** is. It is an optional package where our team takes your feedback and makes the movie happen. You bring the script or the brief, you give all the feedback you want, and it gets accomplished — and you can use us for **part or all** of your video production team. You are not handing over your project to a black box; you are adding capacity to a production that still lives in your own workspace.

## When outsourcing AI video makes sense

Adopting an AI production platform is a real skill investment. The platform is designed for storytellers rather than engineers, but it is still professional software with script structure, cinematography metadata, casting, sets, blocking and render management. Teams commonly hit one of these walls:

- **No bandwidth.** The team is two people and already shipping five campaigns a quarter.
- **No time to learn a new pipeline** before the deadline that made you look for one.
- **Volume spike.** A launch needs 30 assets in three weeks, and the baseline is four.
- **A one-off big swing.** A brand film or a pilot that does not justify hiring for.
- **Proof before commitment.** You want to see what the platform can do on *your* material before you staff around it.

If two or more of those are true, buying execution is usually cheaper than building the muscle mid-flight.

## What the Level 2 team does

The core promise is simple: **you give feedback, we make it happen.** In practice that covers the operating work of an AI production:

- **Script and story setup** — importing your script or expanding a brief into beats, scenes and dialogue at the target duration.
- **Shot planning** — building the shot list with cinematography metadata: camera, lens, movement, framing.
- **Casting and wardrobe** — assigning digital actors, building character sheets with the right outfits, setting up per-character consistency.
- **Sets and blocking** — choosing or building locations, placing characters and cameras on the top-down canvas, defining movement paths.
- **Generation at volume** — running batches across multiple engines and managing the render queue and quality tiers.
- **Assembly and delivery** — automatic scene and episode assembly with transitions and audio, and export in the formats you need.
- **Iteration** — taking your notes on the cut and applying them at the source, then rebuilding.

You can take all of that or any slice of it. Many teams keep creative direction and script in-house and hand off the production operating entirely — which is exactly the "part or all of your team" model.

## Why this beats a generic outsourcing arrangement

**The team works inside ACT 3, and so can you.** The production lives in your Organization — the workspace that legally owns the projects and generated assets. Granular permissions (Read, Modify/Edit, Run AI, Use Credits, Billing, Owner) mean you decide exactly what level of access anyone has, including who is allowed to spend credits. You can watch the work happen, comment on it, and take the keys whenever you want.

**Feedback lands at the source, not on the surface.** Because ACT 3 automates the whole pipeline — auto-generated per-shot prompts and first frames, character sheets with correct wardrobe, automated assembly — a note goes back into the script, the story factors or the blocking and propagates. Teams running this way can rebuild an entire film daily from the prior day's feedback rather than patching individual shots.

**Nothing is stranded.** Version-controlled collaboration with full change history, lock-down controls for approved material, and standard exports (EDL, MP4/MOV, ProRes) with handoff to Premiere Pro and DaVinci Resolve mean the work is portable from day one.

**Cost is visible.** ACT 3 is a metered SaaS product: every credit-consuming action shows its cost before it runs, the render queue displays predicted spend for approval, and the Billing permission keeps financial control where it belongs.

A good starting split for a stretched marketing team: you own the brief, the script and the notes; we own everything between the script and the delivered cut. As your team learns the platform, move the boundary.

An optional done-for-you package in which our team operates ACT 3 AI on your production. You give the script and the feedback; we take that feedback into the movie and make it happen — for part or all of your video production team.

**Can I use the Level 2 team for only part of a project?**
Yes. That is the design. Use us for the whole production, for a single sequence, for a launch spike, or just for the operating work while you keep creative direction in-house.

**Who owns the video we produce together?**
Your Organization does. The Organization workspace legally owns the projects and generated assets per the Terms of Service, and ownership can be transferred between members.

**Can my team stay involved while you work?**
Yes. The production sits in your workspace with role-based permissions, version-controlled collaboration and full change history, so your people can review, comment and edit throughout rather than waiting for a delivery.

**What do we need to provide?**
At minimum a brief or a script, your brand and creative direction, and a decision-maker to give notes on each cut. The more specific the direction, the faster the loop.

**Can we take the production in-house later?**
Yes — that is one of the main advantages over generic outsourcing. The project is already in your ACT 3 Organization, so bringing it in-house is a permissions change, not a migration.

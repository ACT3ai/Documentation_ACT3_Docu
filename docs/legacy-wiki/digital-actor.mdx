## Overview

**[Digital Actors](/docs/legacy-wiki/digital-actors "Digital Actors")** in [ACT 3 AI](/docs/legacy-wiki/act3ai "ACT3AI") are fully customizable, AI-generated performers that bring your [scenes](/docs/legacy-wiki/scenes "Scenes") and [shots](/docs/legacy-wiki/shots "Shots") to life.
Instead of casting physical actors, you can design, direct, and reuse digital counterparts that match your [script](/docs/legacy-wiki/script-editor "Script Editor") and [story beats](/docs/legacy-wiki/story-beats "Story Beats").

Digital Actors integrate seamlessly into your [projects](/docs/legacy-wiki/new-project "New Project") and [Editor workspace](/docs/legacy-wiki/editor "Editor"), saving time and costs while maintaining creative flexibility.

## Key Capabilities

- **Full Customization:** Adjust [character](/docs/legacy-wiki/characters "Characters") appearance, wardrobe, hairstyle, ethnicity, and emotional tone.
- **AI Performance Control:** Direct body language, expressions, and timing.
- **Voice & Dubbing:** Assign AI-generated voices with multi-language support and lip-sync accuracy.
- **Motion Capture:** Use mobile devices or external rigs to capture live performances (see [Motion Capture Integration](/docs/legacy-wiki/motion-capture "Motion Capture")).
- **Reusability:** Save actors to your [asset library](/docs/legacy-wiki/asset-library-2 "Asset Library") and reuse them across episodes, projects, or franchises.
- **Rights & Licensing:** Built-in protection ensures actor likenesses are secure for commercial projects.

## How to Create and Use Digital Actors

1. From the [Editor sidebar](/docs/legacy-wiki/editor "Editor"), click **Digital Actors**.
2. Browse the [actor library](/docs/legacy-wiki/actor-library-2 "Actor Library") or select **Create New**.
3. Configure:
   1. Name and [role](/docs/legacy-wiki/characters "Characters") in the story.
   2. Wardrobe, props, and style.
   3. Visual features (face, body proportions, hair).
   4. Performance tone (calm, energetic, fearful, etc.).
4. Assign your Digital Actor to lines in the [script editor](/docs/legacy-wiki/script-editor "Script Editor") or place them directly into [scenes](/docs/legacy-wiki/scenes "Scenes") via [Top-Down View](/docs/legacy-wiki/top-down-view "Top Down View").
5. Preview the actor in [Storyboard panels](/docs/legacy-wiki/storyboard-panels "Storyboard Panels") or generate test clips.

## Workflow Integration

- [AI Wizard](/docs/legacy-wiki/ai-wizard "AI Wizard") – suggests traits and backstory aligned with plot arcs.
- [Editor](/docs/legacy-wiki/editor "Editor") – places actors into scenes with camera and lighting setup.
- [Timeline](/docs/legacy-wiki/timeline "Timeline") – syncs actor performance with dialogue and scene timing.
- [Build Video](/docs/legacy-wiki/build-video "Build Video") – final rendering with selected AI engines ([Google\_Veo\_3](/docs/legacy-wiki/google-veo-3 "Google Veo 3"), [Runway](/docs/legacy-wiki/runway "Runway"), [WAN AI](/docs/legacy-wiki/wan "Wan")).

## Best Practices

- Define [characters](/docs/legacy-wiki/characters "Characters") early to ensure consistency.
- Pair voices with emotion settings for natural delivery.
- Use [motion capture](/docs/legacy-wiki/motion-capture "Motion Capture") for realism in action scenes.
- Lock approved performances to prevent accidental edits in team projects.

## Troubleshooting

- **Actor looks inconsistent across scenes** → Check locked presets in [actor library](/docs/legacy-wiki/actor-library-2 "Actor Library").
- **Lip-sync issues** → Re-render with voice-sync enabled.
- **Performance doesn’t match script tone** → Adjust emotional tone sliders or regenerate.

## Overview

- - Indie Filmmakers** use [ACT 3 AI](/docs/legacy-wiki/act3ai "ACT3AI") to turn small budgets into big-screen visions.

The platform enables independent creators to **produce studio-quality previsualizations, storyboards, and rendered sequences** without relying on expensive locations, crews, or equipment — allowing more time and energy to focus on storytelling.

ACT 3 AI supports every stage of the creative pipeline, from concept to near-final cut, making it ideal for short films, feature-length projects, film festival entries, or proof-of-concept pitches.

## Why ACT 3 AI for Indie Filmmakers?

- **Budget-Friendly:** Reduce production costs by replacing costly physical setups with high-quality AI-generated visuals.
- **Creative Freedom:** Explore multiple scene variations and styles without the cost of reshoots.
- **Faster Turnaround:** Go from concept to preview in hours instead of weeks.
- **Self-Sufficiency:** Handle writing, directing, cinematography, and editing within a single platform.

## Key Features for Indie Filmmakers

- **[New Project](/docs/legacy-wiki/new-project "New Project")** – Start your film project with custom genres, tones, and target formats.
- **[Script Editor](/docs/legacy-wiki/script-editor "Script Editor")** – Format, edit, and refine screenplays with built-in automation.
- **[AI Writing](/docs/legacy-wiki/ai-writing "AI Writing")** – Expand outlines or loglines into full scripts.
- **[AI Directing](/docs/legacy-wiki/ai-directing "AI Directing")** – Set pacing, camera moves, and scene tone with AI-assisted control.
- **[Cinematography](/docs/legacy-wiki/cinematography "Cinematography")** – Experiment with virtual lenses, rigs, and lighting setups.
- **[Storyboard & Panels](/docs/legacy-wiki/storyboard-panels "Storyboard Panels")** – Plan out each shot visually before rendering.
- **[Set Design](/docs/legacy-wiki/set-design "Set Design")** – Create virtual locations and props without building physical sets.
- **[Digital Actors](/docs/legacy-wiki/digital-actors "Digital Actors")** – Test casting choices, costumes, and performances.
- **[Build Video](/docs/legacy-wiki/build-video "Build Video")** – Render sequences in different quality levels for final assembly.

## Example Use Cases

- Producing a **festival short film** with complete visual previews before shooting.
- Creating a **proof-of-concept teaser** to attract investors or collaborators.
- Experimenting with **different endings** for audience testing.
- Building **animated storyboards** to speed up on-set production.

## Workflow

1. Create a [New Project](/docs/legacy-wiki/new-project "New Project") and choose “Indie Film” as the project type.
2. Use [AI Writing](/docs/legacy-wiki/ai-writing "AI Writing") or import your script via [Import Script](/docs/legacy-wiki/import-script "Import Script").
3. Build out your acts and beats in [AI Wizard](/docs/legacy-wiki/ai-wizard "AI Wizard").
4. Add [digital actors](/docs/legacy-wiki/digital-actors "Digital Actors") and [virtual sets](/docs/legacy-wiki/set-design "Set Design") to your scenes.
5. Experiment with different [camera angles](/docs/legacy-wiki/cinematography "Cinematography") and lighting setups.
6. Use [Build Video](/docs/legacy-wiki/build-video "Build Video") to generate previews or final sequences.

## Overview

The **Storyboard & Panels** feature in [ACT 3 AI](/docs/legacy-wiki/act3ai "ACT3AI") lets you visually map out your story before or during production.
It automatically generates or imports storyboard frames for each [scene](https://wiki.act3ai.com/index.php?title=Scene&action=edit&redlink=1 "Scene (page does not exist)") and [shot](https://wiki.act3ai.com/index.php?title=Shot&action=edit&redlink=1 "Shot (page does not exist)"), linking them directly to your [script lines](/docs/legacy-wiki/script-editor "Script Editor") and [timeline](/docs/legacy-wiki/timeline "Timeline") in the [Editor Workspace](/docs/legacy-wiki/editor "Editor").

You can create storyboards manually, import them from design tools, or generate them using AI models like [Google Veo 3](/docs/legacy-wiki/google-veo-3 "Google Veo 3"), [Runway Integration](/docs/legacy-wiki/runway "Runway"), or [WAN AI Integration](/docs/legacy-wiki/wan "Wan").

## Key Features

- **Script-Linked Panels:** Every panel is tied to a [shot](/docs/legacy-wiki/shots "Shots") or [scene](/docs/legacy-wiki/scenes "Scenes") in your script for perfect continuity.
- **AI Panel Generation:** Create storyboard frames instantly from [shot prompts](/docs/legacy-wiki/shot-prompting "Shot Prompting") or scene descriptions.
- **Drag-and-Drop Layout:** Rearrange panels within scenes or across acts.
- **Multi-View Support:** See script text, shot data, and panels side-by-side.
- **Export Options:** Download as PDF, image sheets, or upload to collaboration platforms.

## How to Create Storyboards

1. Open your project in the [Editor](/docs/legacy-wiki/editor "Editor") and switch one column to **Storyboard & Panels** view.
2. Select a [scene](/docs/legacy-wiki/scenes "Scenes") or [shot](/docs/legacy-wiki/shots "Shots") in the outline panel.
3. Click **+ Add Panel**:
   1. **Manual Upload:** Import PNG, JPG, or SVG drawings from your local files.
   2. **AI Generate:** Choose a render engine ([Google\_Veo\_3](/docs/legacy-wiki/google-veo-3 "Google Veo 3"), [Runway](/docs/legacy-wiki/runway "Runway"), [Wan](/docs/legacy-wiki/wan "Wan")) and enter your visual prompt.
   3. **From Script:** Let ACT 3 AI auto-generate from the corresponding [script lines](/docs/legacy-wiki/script-editor "Script Editor").
4. Arrange panels in sequence and link them to their respective shots.

## Panel Editing

- Rename panels for clarity (e.g., "CU – Hero's Eyes").
- Add [camera movement](/docs/legacy-wiki/camera-movement "Camera Movement") or lighting notes to each panel.
- Attach reference images or location assets for production planning.
- Delete or replace panels without affecting the [script](/docs/legacy-wiki/script-editor "Script Editor").

## AI-Generated Panels

- AI generation consumes [credits](/docs/legacy-wiki/credit-system "Credit System") based on resolution and model.
- Models:
  - [Google Veo 3](/docs/legacy-wiki/google-veo-3 "Google Veo 3") – Realistic/photographic previews.
  - [Runway Integration](/docs/legacy-wiki/runway "Runway") – Stylized/experimental looks.
  - [WAN AI Integration](/docs/legacy-wiki/wan "Wan") – Fast low-latency previews for collaboration.
- Adjust prompt style, camera angle, lighting, and tone for each shot.

## Collaboration

- Co-edit storyboards in real time with your team.
- Comment directly on panels to suggest changes.
- Lock panels once approved to prevent accidental edits.

## Exporting

- Export entire storyboards as:
  - PDF – Printable, client-ready format.
  - PNG/JPG sheets – For quick sharing.
  - Linked media – Embed in [documentation](/docs/legacy-wiki/documentation "Documentation") or pitch decks.
- Maintain script-panel links on export for easy reference.

## Tips for Best Results

- Keep panels clear and focused on one action or moment.
- Maintain visual continuity – ensure characters, sets, and props match across panels.
- Use panels as a communication tool between writers, directors, and production teams.

## Troubleshooting

- **Panel not linked to shot** → Drag the panel thumbnail onto the target shot in the outline.
- **AI result off-target** → Refine your [prompt](/docs/legacy-wiki/shot-prompting "Shot Prompting") or change the render engine.
- **Missing assets in export** → Ensure all panels are approved and saved before exporting.

## Overview

The **Preview** feature in [ACT 3 AI](/docs/legacy-wiki/act3ai "ACT3AI") allows creators to generate quick, AI-rendered previews of their projects before committing full resources to rendering.
It bridges the gap between [script editing](/docs/legacy-wiki/script-editing "Script Editing"), [timeline planning](/docs/legacy-wiki/timeline-view "Timeline View"), and [final output](/docs/legacy-wiki/build-video "Build Video")—helping users validate pacing, visuals, and AI prompts in real-time.

## Key Capabilities

- **Quick Renders:** Generate low-resolution previews of scenes or shots directly in the [Editor workspace](/docs/legacy-wiki/editor "Editor").
- **Resolution Options:** Choose from Draft, Medium, High, or 4K preview modes depending on your needs and credit budget.
- **Inline Playback:** Watch previews within the Preview pane or directly inside the [Storyboard view](/docs/legacy-wiki/storyboard-panels "Storyboard Panels").
- **Regeneration:** One-click update of previews after script or shot prompt changes.
- **Cost Awareness:** Shows estimated [credit consumption](/docs/legacy-wiki/credit-system "Credit System") before rendering.

## How to Use

1. Open the [Editor](/docs/legacy-wiki/editor "Editor") and select a shot, scene, or sequence from the [Timeline](/docs/legacy-wiki/timeline-view "Timeline View").
2. Click **Preview → Generate**.
3. Choose your quality level and AI engine (e.g., [Google Veo 3](/docs/legacy-wiki/google-veo-3 "Google Veo 3"), [WAN AI](/docs/legacy-wiki/wan "Wan"), [Runway](/docs/legacy-wiki/runway "Runway")).
4. Watch the preview in the Preview panel.
5. Regenerate if adjustments are made to script, shot prompts, or camera setups.

## Collaboration

- Share previews in [collaborative sessions](/docs/legacy-wiki/collaboration "Collaboration") with teammates.
- Comment and leave feedback inline on the [timeline](/docs/legacy-wiki/timeline-view "Timeline View") or [storyboard](/docs/legacy-wiki/storyboard-panels "Storyboard Panels").
- Lock approved previews to prevent further changes.

## Best Practices

- Use low-resolution previews for early drafts and switch to high quality for final checks.
- Preview small segments before rendering full scenes to save credits.
- Split longer sequences into multiple previews for easier iteration.

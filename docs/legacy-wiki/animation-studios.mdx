## Overview

The **Animation Studios Integration** in [ACT 3 AI](/docs/legacy-wiki/act3ai "ACT3AI") connects your creative workflow with third-party 2D and 3D animation platforms.
It allows studios, freelancers, and production teams to import, manage, and render animated assets directly in the ACT 3 pipeline, reducing manual hand-offs and making the pre-production to final export process seamless.

## Key Capabilities

- **Direct Import:** Bring in animated assets from leading tools like Blender, Maya, Unreal Engine, or Toon Boom.
- **Format Compatibility:** Supports FBX, OBJ, GLTF, Alembic, MP4, and ProRes pipelines.
- **2D & 3D Integration:** Combine hand-drawn animation, digital rigs, and photorealistic 3D environments with ACT 3 generated elements.
- **Collaborative Editing:** Use [Timeline & Storyboard](/docs/legacy-wiki/timeline-and-storyboard "Timeline & Storyboard") to align animation sequences with AI-generated or live-action shots.
- **Cloud Rendering:** Offload heavy rendering tasks to ACT 3’s cloud service or export back to your studio’s render farm.

## How to Use

1. Go to the [Media Library](/docs/legacy-wiki/media-library "Media Library") and click **Import → Animation Asset**.
2. Select from supported formats or connect a partner studio pipeline.
3. Place imported animation in your [Editor workspace](/docs/legacy-wiki/editor "Editor").
4. Sync with [Digital Actors](/docs/legacy-wiki/digital-actors "Digital Actors") or [2D & 3D Sets](/docs/legacy-wiki/2d-and-3d-sets "2D & 3D Sets") for seamless compositing.
5. Render previews in [Render Queue](/docs/legacy-wiki/render-queue "Render Queue") or export final assemblies via [Build Video](/docs/legacy-wiki/build-video "Build Video").

## Example Workflow

| Input | Animation Studio Contribution | Next Step |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Storyboard panel with action cue | 3D rig imported from Maya | Align in [Timeline & Storyboard](/docs/legacy-wiki/timeline-and-storyboard "Timeline & Storyboard") |
| Scene with [Digital Actor](/docs/legacy-wiki/digital-actors "Digital Actors") dialogue | Lip-synced 2D character from Toon Boom | Merge with AI-generated B-Roll |
| Complex environment | Unreal Engine scene imported as [3D Set](/docs/legacy-wiki/2d-and-3d-sets "2D & 3D Sets") | Apply lighting & camera moves |
| Final cut | Batch rendering with animation + AI assets | Export with [Build Video](/docs/legacy-wiki/build-video "Build Video") |

## Pricing & Credits

- Animation imports do not consume credits.
- Cloud rendering of animation sequences consumes [credits](/docs/legacy-wiki/credit-system "Credit System") based on resolution and duration.
- Partner integrations (e.g., Unreal, Blender plugins) may require external licensing.

## Troubleshooting & Tips

- Ensure your source animation uses supported frame rates (24, 30, 60 fps).
- For heavy 3D environments, use [WAN AI previews](/docs/legacy-wiki/wan "Wan") before final rendering.
- Always keep naming conventions consistent to avoid broken links in [Media Library](/docs/legacy-wiki/media-library "Media Library").

## Overview

In [ACT 3 AI](/docs/legacy-wiki/act3ai "ACT3AI"), **Cameras** define how scenes and shots are framed, captured, and rendered.
By adjusting camera settings, you can control angles, movements, lenses, depth of field, and cinematic style — giving your project professional film language.

The camera system works in tandem with [Camera Instructions](/docs/legacy-wiki/camera-instructions "Camera Instructions"), [Camera Angles](/docs/legacy-wiki/camera-angles "Camera Angles"), and [Camera Movement](/docs/legacy-wiki/camera-movement "Camera Movement") to provide full cinematography control.

## Key Capabilities

- **Angles:** High angle, low angle, POV, over-the-shoulder, Dutch tilt, etc.
- **Movements:** Dolly, pan, tilt, tracking, handheld, crane, Steadicam, drone.
- **Lens Settings:** Wide-angle, telephoto, fisheye, aperture (f-stops).
- **Focus:** Shallow vs. deep focus, rack focus.
- **Framing:** Close-up, medium shot, wide shot, establishing shot.
- **Style & Mood:** Cinematic descriptors such as noir, documentary, handheld, or surreal.

## How Cameras Work in ACT 3 AI

1. Open your [Editor workspace](/docs/legacy-wiki/editor "Editor").
2. Select a [scene](/docs/legacy-wiki/scenes "Scenes") or [shot](/docs/legacy-wiki/shots "Shots").
3. Add [shot prompts](/docs/legacy-wiki/shot-prompting "Shot Prompting") that include camera details (e.g., “low-angle dolly shot, dramatic lighting”).
4. Preview the camera setup in [Storyboards](/docs/legacy-wiki/storyboard-panels "Storyboard Panels") or [Top-Down View](/docs/legacy-wiki/top-down-view "Top Down View").
5. Render through [Google Veo 3](/docs/legacy-wiki/google-veo-3 "Google Veo 3"), [Runway Integration](/docs/legacy-wiki/runway "Runway"), or [WAN AI Integration](/docs/legacy-wiki/wan "Wan").

## Camera Types

- **Fixed Camera:** Stationary shot, no movement.
- **Dynamic Camera:** Uses [camera movement](/docs/legacy-wiki/camera-movement "Camera Movement") like pans, tilts, and dollies.
- **Virtual Drone:** Overhead or aerial sweeping shots.
- **POV Camera:** Mimics a character’s point of view.
- **Multi-Camera Setup:** Use multiple virtual cameras to cover a scene from different angles.

## Example Camera Prompts

- “Wide establishing shot, drone aerial view of a desert city.”
- “Close-up of hero’s face, handheld shaky cam.”
- “Over-the-shoulder POV as detective examines evidence, shallow focus.”
- “Slow dolly in on villain, low angle, dramatic shadows.”

## Best Practices

- Match camera type to narrative tone (e.g., handheld for chaos, Steadicam for smooth action).
- Use [angles](/docs/legacy-wiki/camera-angles "Camera Angles") + [movement](/docs/legacy-wiki/camera-movement "Camera Movement") together for cinematic variety.
- Keep camera prompts short and precise for better AI interpretation.
- Plan camera paths in [Top-Down View](/docs/legacy-wiki/top-down-view "Top Down View") for complex blocking.

## Troubleshooting

- **Unclear framing** → Use precise film terms (e.g., “medium close-up” instead of “zoom on face”).
- **Ignored movement** → Split complex instructions into multiple [shots](/docs/legacy-wiki/shots "Shots").
- **Jittery handheld look** → Specify “light handheld” or switch to Steadicam.

## Overview

The **Getting Started** section of the ACT 3 AI platform helps new users quickly begin their creative journey. From launching a project to rendering your first video, this guide walks through the essential steps needed to create content with AI support.

## Main Steps

The workflow consists of key stages, each accessible via the sidebar:

**[New Project](/docs/legacy-wiki/new-project "New Project")** – Begin a new production by choosing a format and naming your project.

**Write or Import a Script** – Use the built-in editor or bring in a script from another tool.

**[AI Wizard](/docs/legacy-wiki/ai-wizard "AI Wizard")** – Generate story structure, beats, and arcs using the AI Wizard assistant.

**[Digital Actors](/docs/legacy-wiki/digital-actors "Digital Actors")** – Select and customize AI-generated characters for your scenes.

**[Editor](/docs/legacy-wiki/editor "Editor")** – Use the Top‑Down View and Storyboard tools to organize your production visually.

**Render Previews** – Generate AI-rendered videos in different qualities and formats.

## [New Project](/docs/legacy-wiki/new-project "New Project")

- Click **[New Project](/docs/legacy-wiki/new-project "New Project")** from the dashboard or sidebar.
- Fill in the project name and description.
- Select your content type (short film, ad, TikTok, concept test).
- Save and proceed to script development.

## Write or Import a Script

- Use the built-in script editor to write directly in ACT 3 AI.
- Automatically formats headers, characters, and dialogue.
- Import text from Final Draft, Markdown, or plain text.
- AI Workers can help expand outlines into fully structured scenes.

## [AI Wizard](/docs/legacy-wiki/ai-wizard "AI Wizard")

- Open the **AI Wizard** from the sidebar.
- Choose your genre, tone, and narrative focus.
- Review auto-generated arcs, acts, and scenes.
- Convert beats into script sections for editing.

## [Digital Actors](/docs/legacy-wiki/digital-actors "Digital Actors")

- Access the digital actor library or create new ones.
- Customize character appearance and assign to script lines.
- Capture facial expressions or motion using your phone.

## [Editor](/docs/legacy-wiki/editor "Editor") – Plan Scenes Visually

- Use the **Top‑Down View** to place cameras, props, and actors on a virtual map.
- Use **Storyboard & Panels** to visualize each shot.
- Adjust camera paths, timing, and lighting settings.

## Render Previews

- Select scenes or shots to render.
- Choose quality: Draft, Medium, High, or 4K.
- Pick a rendering engine ([Google\_Veo\_3](/docs/legacy-wiki/google-veo-3 "Google Veo 3"), [Wan](/docs/legacy-wiki/wan "Wan"), [Runway](/docs/legacy-wiki/runway "Runway")).
- Preview before committing credits and regenerate as needed.

## Manage Credits and Plan

- See estimated credits before rendering.
- View your credit balance at the top of the screen.
- Upgrade your plan from the sidebar or visit [Subscription Plan](/docs/legacy-wiki/subscription-plan "Subscription Plan").

## Collaboration

- Invite team members to your Organization.
- Collaborate in real-time on scripts, scenes, and previews.
- Lock scenes when finalized to prevent changes.
- Use inline comments for team feedback.

## Getting Help

- Press **F1** or click the **Help** icon in any view.
- Hover over any tool for instant tooltips.
- Visit [Editor](/docs/legacy-wiki/editor "Editor") and [AI Wizard](/docs/legacy-wiki/ai-wizard "AI Wizard") for deeper guidance.

[Contact Us](https://act3ai.com/contact) if you need additional support or have product questions.

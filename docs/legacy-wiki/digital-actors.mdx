## Overview

**Digital Actors** in [ACT 3 AI](/docs/legacy-wiki/act3ai "ACT3AI") are AI-generated characters that can perform scripted roles within your productions.
They combine realistic visual rendering, customizable personalities, and controllable performance styles, making them essential for both narrative and commercial content.

Digital Actors can be used in [storyboards](/docs/legacy-wiki/timeline-and-storyboard "Timeline & Storyboard"), [shot prompts](/docs/legacy-wiki/shot-prompting "Shot Prompting"), and final [build videos](/docs/legacy-wiki/build-video "Build Video"), replacing the need for traditional casting while preserving creative control.

## Key Capabilities

- **Character Library:** Choose from a catalog of pre-designed archetypes (heroes, villains, narrators, etc.).
- **Customization:** Adjust [character attributes](/docs/legacy-wiki/characters "Characters")—age, gender, ethnicity, wardrobe, voice, and movement style.
- **Performance Direction:** Control body language, tone, and emotional intensity through [AI Wizard](/docs/legacy-wiki/ai-wizard "AI Wizard") prompts.
- **Voice & Lip Sync:** Pair with [audio tracks](/docs/legacy-wiki/audio-tracks "Audio Tracks") for synced dialogue and natural expressions.
- **Cross-Scene Continuity:** Keep the same Digital Actor consistent across multiple [scenes](/docs/legacy-wiki/scenes "Scenes") or even entire [projects](/docs/legacy-wiki/projects "Projects").
- **Multi-Actor Interaction:** Place multiple actors together with collision-aware staging and dynamic [camera angles](/docs/legacy-wiki/camera-angles "Camera Angles").

## How to Use

1. Open the [Editor workspace](/docs/legacy-wiki/editor "Editor") and select **Digital Actor → Add Actor**.
2. Browse the available catalog or import a saved preset.
3. Customize appearance (hair, clothing, accessories) and attributes (age, height, style).
4. Add scripted dialogue or link to [imported scripts](/docs/legacy-wiki/import-script "Import Script").
5. Position the actor within [2D or 3D sets](/docs/legacy-wiki/2d-and-3d-sets "2D & 3D Sets").
6. Preview in [Top Down View](/docs/legacy-wiki/top-down-view "Top Down View") and finalize in the [video build](/docs/legacy-wiki/build-video "Build Video").

## Example Workflow

| Step | Action | Output |
| --- | --- | --- |
| 1 | Import a [script](/docs/legacy-wiki/script-editor "Script Editor") | Scene dialogue linked to Digital Actor |
| 2 | Add Digital Actor (e.g., “Detective” preset) | Character model placed in set |
| 3 | Customize attributes (age 42, trench coat, gravelly voice) | Unique, tailored character |
| 4 | Assign dialogue and expressions | Actor lip-syncs with emotion |
| 5 | Render preview with [camera movement](/docs/legacy-wiki/camera-movement "Camera Movement") | Cinematic clip with full performance |

## Tips

- Use consistent wardrobe and voice profiles to maintain continuity across episodes.
- Combine facial expression controls with [lighting setups](/docs/legacy-wiki/lighting "Lighting") for dramatic effect.
- Save frequently used actors into the [Media Library](/docs/legacy-wiki/media-library "Media Library") for reuse.
- For ensemble scenes, stagger body movement settings to avoid robotic uniformity.

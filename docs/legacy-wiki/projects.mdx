## Overview

In [ACT 3 AI](/docs/legacy-wiki/act3ai "ACT3AI"), a **Project** is the central container that holds all of your creative work — from [scripts](/docs/legacy-wiki/scripts "Scripts") and [characters](/docs/legacy-wiki/characters "Characters") to [digital actors](/docs/legacy-wiki/digital-actors "Digital Actors"), [scenes](/docs/legacy-wiki/scenes "Scenes"), [shots](/docs/legacy-wiki/shots "Shots"), and final [video assemblies](/docs/legacy-wiki/build-video "Build Video").
Every production you create (a short film, ad, TikTok, or feature-length movie) begins as a Project.

## Project Structure

Projects in ACT 3 AI are organized in a nested hierarchy:

- **Project → Acts → Scenes → Shots → Assets (Video, Audio, B-Roll, etc.)**

This structure ensures that everything from idea to final export is traceable and editable in one place.

## Creating a Project

1. From the dashboard, select **[New Project](/docs/legacy-wiki/new-project "New Project")**.
2. Enter a project name, description, and content type (Film, Ad, TikTok, Explainer).
3. Choose aspect ratio (16:9, 9:16, 1:1) and estimated duration.
4. Save to begin working in the [Editor workspace](/docs/legacy-wiki/editor "Editor").

## Project Components

- [Script Editor](/docs/legacy-wiki/script-editor "Script Editor") – where dialogue and action are written or imported.
- [AI Wizard](/docs/legacy-wiki/ai-wizard "AI Wizard") – generates acts, beats, and story arcs.
- [Characters](/docs/legacy-wiki/characters "Characters") & [Digital Actors](/docs/legacy-wiki/digital-actors "Digital Actors") – define and cast your story.
- [Storyboards & Panels](/docs/legacy-wiki/storyboard-panels "Storyboard Panels") – visualize the project flow.
- [Timeline](/docs/legacy-wiki/timeline "Timeline") – sequence and edit your shots.
- [Build Video](/docs/legacy-wiki/build-video "Build Video") – final assembly of the project.
- [Render Queue](/docs/legacy-wiki/render-queue "Render Queue") – handles rendering jobs and exports.

## Managing Projects

- **Folders:** Organize projects by client, department, or campaign.
- **Versioning:** Duplicate projects to branch alternate edits.
- **Collaboration:** Share projects with your [organization team](/docs/legacy-wiki/organization "Organization").
- **Locking:** Freeze finished acts or scenes to prevent accidental edits.
- **Archiving:** Archive inactive projects for storage efficiency.

## Credits & Billing

- Projects consume credits when rendering, previewing, or exporting.
- Credit use depends on project size, resolution, and rendering engine (see [Billing Model](/docs/legacy-wiki/billing-model "Billing Model")).
- Admins can monitor project-level credit usage in [Credit Dashboard](/docs/legacy-wiki/credit-system "Credit System").

## Exporting Projects

Projects can be exported through [Export Distribution](/docs/legacy-wiki/export-distribution "Export Distribution") into:

- MP4, MOV, ProRes video formats
- Editing formats (EDL, AAF)
- Scripts ([Export Script](/docs/legacy-wiki/export-script "Export Script"))

## Best Practices

- Name projects clearly (include client or campaign tags).
- Use [draft previews](/docs/legacy-wiki/draft-previews "Draft Previews") before committing to final renders.
- Keep [characters](/docs/legacy-wiki/characters "Characters") consistent by reusing actor profiles.
- Review credit usage regularly on long projects.

## Troubleshooting

- **Project won’t load** → Clear cache or check [permissions](/docs/legacy-wiki/organization "Organization").
- **Missing assets** → Confirm uploads via [Import B-Roll](/docs/legacy-wiki/import-broll "Import BRoll") or [Import Script](/docs/legacy-wiki/import-script "Import Script").
- **Out of credits** → Purchase extra via [Subscription Plan](/docs/legacy-wiki/subscription-plan "Subscription Plan").

## Overview

The **Collaboration** features in [ACT 3 AI](/docs/legacy-wiki/act3ai "ACT3AI") enable teams to work together on scripts, [scenes](/docs/legacy-wiki/scenes "Scenes"), [shots](/docs/legacy-wiki/shots "Shots"), and final videos in real time.
Multiple creators, writers, editors, and producers can contribute simultaneously within the [Editor workspace](/docs/legacy-wiki/editor "Editor"), ensuring faster feedback and streamlined production.

Collaboration works best when paired with [Organization Management](/docs/legacy-wiki/organization "Organization") tools, allowing administrators to control permissions, credits, and billing.

## Key Capabilities

- **Real-Time Editing:** Multiple team members can edit [scripts](/docs/legacy-wiki/scripts "Scripts"), [timelines](/docs/legacy-wiki/timeline "Timeline"), and [storyboards](/docs/legacy-wiki/storyboard-panels "Storyboard Panels") at the same time.
- **Presence Awareness:** See who is currently active in each workspace.
- **Comments & Annotations:** Leave notes on specific [script lines](/docs/legacy-wiki/script-editor "Script Editor"), [timeline points](/docs/legacy-wiki/timeline "Timeline"), or [previews](/docs/legacy-wiki/preview "Preview").
- **Roles & Permissions:** Assign roles such as writer, director, editor, or reviewer within your [organization](/docs/legacy-wiki/organization "Organization").
- **Content Locking:** Lock completed [scenes](/docs/legacy-wiki/scenes "Scenes") or [shots](/docs/legacy-wiki/shots "Shots") to prevent unwanted changes.
- **Version Control:** Track changes, restore previous versions, and approve final drafts.

## How It Works

1. Create or join an [organization](/docs/legacy-wiki/organization "Organization") account.
2. Open a shared [project](/docs/legacy-wiki/project "Project") from the dashboard.
3. Invite collaborators via email or access link.
4. Assign roles (Writer, Editor, Reviewer, Admin).
5. Work together in the [Editor workspace](/docs/legacy-wiki/editor "Editor") with live updates and syncing.
6. Use comments and locks to manage approvals.

## Example Use Cases

- A writing team drafts dialogue while a director reviews [camera instructions](/docs/legacy-wiki/camera-instructions "Camera Instructions").
- An editor refines [timing](/docs/legacy-wiki/timeline "Timeline") while a producer checks [credit usage](/docs/legacy-wiki/credit-system "Credit System").
- A VFX artist uploads assets into [Cloud Storage](/docs/legacy-wiki/cloud-storage-management "Cloud Storage Management") while the team previews renders.

## Integration with Workflow

- [AI Wizard](/docs/legacy-wiki/ai-wizard "AI Wizard") – Teams can brainstorm and refine acts, beats, and loglines together.
- [Script Editor](/docs/legacy-wiki/script-editor "Script Editor") – Writers and editors can work on scripts in real time.
- [Editor workspace](/docs/legacy-wiki/editor "Editor") – Directors and editors manage scene composition and pacing collaboratively.
- [Build Video](/docs/legacy-wiki/build-video "Build Video") – Final video assembly can be reviewed and approved collectively.
- [Organization Management](/docs/legacy-wiki/organization "Organization") – Controls team access, billing, and credit allocation.

## Best Practices

- Use comments for feedback instead of overwriting changes.
- Lock finished content to prevent accidental edits.
- Maintain clear version names (e.g., “Scene\_3\_Draft\_A”).
- Assign specific roles to avoid duplication of work.

## Troubleshooting

- **Changes not appearing** → Refresh editor or check internet connection.
- **Conflicting edits** → Enable content locking and use version control.
- **User cannot access project** → Verify role assignment in [organization settings](/docs/legacy-wiki/organization "Organization").

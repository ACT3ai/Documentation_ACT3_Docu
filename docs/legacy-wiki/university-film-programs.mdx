## Overview

University Film Programs can integrate [ACT 3 AI](/docs/legacy-wiki/act3ai "ACT3AI") into coursework to give students hands-on experience with **AI-powered filmmaking tools**.
The platform allows film and media students to experiment with **scriptwriting, directing, cinematography, and editing** in a virtual environment — removing budget, time, and equipment constraints.

By enabling rapid prototyping and iteration, ACT 3 AI helps students focus on **storytelling fundamentals** while gaining exposure to emerging production technologies used in the professional industry.

## Benefits for Film Schools

- **Low-Cost Learning:** No need for costly cameras, sets, or location rentals.
- **Scalable Access:** All students can work on projects simultaneously without competing for limited school resources.
- **Industry-Relevant Skills:** Hands-on training with the same AI workflows transforming professional filmmaking.
- **Faster Feedback Loops:** Students can iterate quickly between concept, preview, and revision.

## Key Features for University Programs

- **[New Project](/docs/legacy-wiki/new-project "New Project")** – Students can start short films, scenes, or experimental clips in minutes.
- **[Script Editor](/docs/legacy-wiki/script-editor "Script Editor")** – Industry-standard screenplay formatting built into the platform.
- **[AI Writing](/docs/legacy-wiki/ai-writing "AI Writing")** – Assists students in developing ideas into structured scripts.
- **[AI Directing](/docs/legacy-wiki/ai-directing "AI Directing")** – Lets students practice shot composition, pacing, and scene mood.
- **[Cinematography](/docs/legacy-wiki/cinematography "Cinematography")** – Virtual camera and lighting setups for creative experimentation.
- **[Set Design](/docs/legacy-wiki/set-design "Set Design")** – Build digital environments without expensive set construction.
- **[Digital Actors](/docs/legacy-wiki/digital-actors "Digital Actors")** – Assign roles, customize costumes, and experiment with character dynamics.
- **[Build Video](/docs/legacy-wiki/build-video "Build Video")** – Render complete sequences for review or festival submission.

## Example Academic Uses

- **Directing Class:** Assign students to reinterpret the same script with different visual styles.
- **Screenwriting Workshop:** Use [AI Writing](/docs/legacy-wiki/ai-writing "AI Writing") to expand short prompts into multiple script drafts.
- **Cinematography Lab:** Experiment with lenses, lighting, and movement in [Top-Down View](/docs/legacy-wiki/top-down-view "Top Down View").
- **Production Design Course:** Create and present fully designed virtual sets in [Set Design](/docs/legacy-wiki/set-design "Set Design").
- **Capstone Projects:** Develop, render, and present final films entirely within ACT 3 AI.

## Workflow for Classroom Integration

1. Faculty creates a shared [Organization](/docs/legacy-wiki/organization "Organization") account for students.
2. Assign each student or team a [New Project](/docs/legacy-wiki/new-project "New Project").
3. Use [AI Wizard](/docs/legacy-wiki/ai-wizard "AI Wizard") to develop plot structure and beats.
4. Build scenes using [Storyboard Panels](/docs/legacy-wiki/storyboard-panels "Storyboard Panels") and [Digital Actors](/docs/legacy-wiki/digital-actors "Digital Actors").
5. Render final sequences with [Build Video](/docs/legacy-wiki/build-video "Build Video") for screening and critique.

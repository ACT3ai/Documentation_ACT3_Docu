## Overview

The **Visual Style** settings in [ACT 3 AI](/docs/legacy-wiki/act3ai "ACT3AI") allow creators to define the overall look, mood, and aesthetic of their video project.
By selecting a style profile, you influence how the AI renders colors, lighting, textures, and artistic tone across [scenes](/docs/legacy-wiki/scenes "Scenes"), [shots](/docs/legacy-wiki/shots "Shots"), and [set designs](/docs/legacy-wiki/set-design "Set Design").

Visual styles can be applied globally to an entire project or selectively to individual sequences for varied artistic expression.

## Key Capabilities

- **Preset Styles:** Choose from cinematic, photorealistic, animation, stylized, noir, vintage, and other predefined looks.
- **Custom Styles:** Create your own by adjusting color grading, lighting profiles, texture sharpness, and depth of field.
- **Model-Specific Styles:** Some integrations like [Google Veo 3](/docs/legacy-wiki/google-veo-3 "Google Veo 3") and [Hunyuan Integration](/docs/legacy-wiki/hunyuan "Hunyuan") offer exclusive style presets.
- **Scene-Level Control:** Override the global style for specific [scenes](/docs/legacy-wiki/scenes "Scenes") or [shots](/docs/legacy-wiki/shots "Shots").
- **Style Persistence:** Maintain consistent visual tone across episodes or multi-part productions.

## How to Set Visual Style

1. From the [New Project](/docs/legacy-wiki/new-project "New Project") creation screen, select your desired style in the **Visual Style** dropdown.
2. If editing an existing project, open the [Editor workspace](/docs/legacy-wiki/editor "Editor") and click the **Visual Style** panel.
3. Browse available presets or create a custom configuration.
4. Apply the style globally or to specific [scenes](/docs/legacy-wiki/scenes "Scenes") and [shots](/docs/legacy-wiki/shots "Shots").
5. Preview changes instantly using the Preview panel or [Render Queue](/docs/legacy-wiki/render-queue "Render Queue").

## Tips & Best Practices

- Choose a style that matches your project’s genre (e.g., cinematic for drama, bold colors for animation).
- Combine with [cinematography controls](/docs/legacy-wiki/cinematography "Cinematography") to fine-tune lighting and camera angles.
- Test multiple styles on the same [shot](/docs/legacy-wiki/shots "Shots") to compare results before rendering the full project.
- Maintain style consistency for branding and visual identity.

## Example Use Cases

- A noir crime short film with high-contrast monochrome lighting.
- A fantasy animation with painterly textures and vibrant colors.
- A corporate video with neutral tones and clean compositions.
- A horror teaser using desaturated colors and heavy shadows.

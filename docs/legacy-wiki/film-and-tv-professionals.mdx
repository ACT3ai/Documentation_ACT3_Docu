## Overview

Film and TV Professionals — including writers, directors, producers, and post-production teams — use [ACT 3 AI](/docs/legacy-wiki/act3ai "ACT3AI") to streamline pre-production, accelerate concept visualization, and reduce the cost of producing high-quality sequences.
The platform allows professionals to **experiment quickly**, **visualize scripts**, and **generate production-ready assets** without the need for large physical sets or crews during early stages.

## Why ACT 3 AI for Film & TV?

- **Faster Pre-Production:** Convert scripts into visual storyboards, shot lists, and previews in hours instead of weeks.
- **Cost Efficiency:** Reduce expenses for location scouting, set building, and reshoots.
- **Creative Freedom:** Quickly explore multiple versions of scenes, angles, and styles before committing to filming.
- **Collaboration:** Work with distributed teams in real time on the same project.

## Key Features for Film and TV Professionals

- **[Script Editor](/docs/legacy-wiki/script-editor "Script Editor")** – Format and refine screenplays with industry-standard formatting.
- **[AI Directing](/docs/legacy-wiki/ai-directing "AI Directing")** – Adjust pacing, tone, and shot composition with AI assistance.
- **[Cinematography](/docs/legacy-wiki/cinematography "Cinematography")** – Simulate professional camera rigs, lighting setups, and cinematic framing.
- **[Storyboard & Panels](/docs/legacy-wiki/storyboard-panels "Storyboard Panels")** – Visualize key shots before live-action production.
- **[Set Design](/docs/legacy-wiki/set-design "Set Design")** – Build virtual sets for previsualization.
- **[Digital Actors](/docs/legacy-wiki/digital-actors "Digital Actors")** – Test performances, costumes, and blocking with AI-generated talent.
- **[Top-Down View](/docs/legacy-wiki/top-down-view "Top Down View")** – Map camera positions, actor placements, and props.
- **[Render Queue](/docs/legacy-wiki/render-queue "Render Queue")** – Generate high-quality renders for pre-production review.

## Example Use Cases

- Pitching a **new TV pilot** with AI-generated scene mockups.
- Previsualizing complex **VFX sequences** before post-production.
- Testing different **storyboard flows** for a film’s climax.
- Producing **animatics** for investor presentations.

## Workflow

1. Start a [New Project](/docs/legacy-wiki/new-project "New Project") and select "Film/TV" as the project type.
2. Import your script via [Import Script](/docs/legacy-wiki/import-script "Import Script") or paste directly into the [Script Editor](/docs/legacy-wiki/script-editor "Script Editor").
3. Use [AI Wizard](/docs/legacy-wiki/ai-wizard "AI Wizard") to structure acts and beats.
4. Build out [storyboards](/docs/legacy-wiki/storyboard-panels "Storyboard Panels") and adjust [camera setups](/docs/legacy-wiki/cinematography "Cinematography").
5. Customize [digital actors](/docs/legacy-wiki/digital-actors "Digital Actors") and [sets](/docs/legacy-wiki/set-design "Set Design").
6. Render preview scenes for review using [Google Veo 3](/docs/legacy-wiki/google-veo-3 "Google Veo 3"), [Runway Integration](/docs/legacy-wiki/runway "Runway"), or [WAN AI](/docs/legacy-wiki/wan "Wan").

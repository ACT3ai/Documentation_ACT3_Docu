## Overview

The **AI Writing** feature in [ACT 3 AI](/docs/legacy-wiki/act3ai "ACT3AI") helps creators expand simple ideas into complete, production-ready screenplays.
It leverages advanced generative models to produce [beats](/docs/legacy-wiki/story-beats "Story Beats"), [scenes](/docs/legacy-wiki/scenes "Scenes"), and dialogue, while keeping the writer in full creative control.

With AI Writing, you can move from a one-line concept to a fully structured [script](/docs/legacy-wiki/script-editor "Script Editor") in minutes.

## Key Capabilities

- **Idea Expansion:** Grow a short prompt into acts, scenes, and dialogue.
- **Beat Generation:** Automatically generate [story beats](/docs/legacy-wiki/story-beats "Story Beats") for each act.
- **Draft Writing:** Create structured [scripts](/docs/legacy-wiki/scripts "Scripts") with character names, dialogue, and scene headings.
- **Style & Tone:** Adjust genre, pacing, and narrative voice to match your project.
- **Human-in-the-loop Editing:** Compare versions, accept or reject AI suggestions, and refine.

## How It Works

1. Start with a short description or logline in [New Project](/docs/legacy-wiki/new-project "New Project").
2. Open the [AI Wizard](/docs/legacy-wiki/ai-wizard "AI Wizard") to define genre, tone, theme, and audience.
3. AI generates a structured outline with [acts](/docs/legacy-wiki/acts "Acts"), [scenes](/docs/legacy-wiki/scenes "Scenes"), and [beats](/docs/legacy-wiki/story-beats "Story Beats").
4. Expand beats into dialogue using the [Script Editor](/docs/legacy-wiki/script-editor "Script Editor").
5. Revise and refine manually, or regenerate specific lines with AI assistance.

## Example Workflow

- Input: “A lone astronaut explores an alien planet.”
- Output:

:   Act 1 — Arrival on the planet, first signs of alien life.
:   Act 2 — Rising tension, discovery of alien structures.
:   Act 3 — Climax and revelation, astronaut escapes or makes first contact.

- Script is formatted automatically with headings, dialogue, and stage directions.

## Benefits

- **Speed:** Generate a draft script in minutes.
- **Flexibility:** Explore multiple variations of tone, character, or dialogue.
- **Consistency:** Maintain formatting standards for [Final Draft](/docs/legacy-wiki/final-draft "Final Draft") or [Export Script](/docs/legacy-wiki/export-script "Export Script").
- **Collaboration:** Multiple writers can edit AI drafts in real time.

## Integration with Workflow

- [AI Wizard](/docs/legacy-wiki/ai-wizard "AI Wizard") → High-level story arcs and beats.
- [Script Editor](/docs/legacy-wiki/script-editor "Script Editor") → Write, edit, and format full scripts.
- [Characters](/docs/legacy-wiki/characters "Characters") → Link dialogue directly to character profiles.
- [Digital Actors](/docs/legacy-wiki/digital-actors "Digital Actors") → Assign AI actors to scripted dialogue.
- [Timeline](/docs/legacy-wiki/timeline "Timeline") → Map generated scripts to scene pacing.

## Best Practices

- Provide detailed prompts (genre, setting, characters) for stronger outputs.
- Use AI to explore multiple versions before committing.
- Always review and refine AI drafts for tone and accuracy.
- Break long scripts into smaller sections for easier AI processing.

## Troubleshooting

- **Generic dialogue** → Add character notes or traits in [character profiles](/docs/legacy-wiki/characters "Characters").
- **Wrong genre/tone** → Re-run generation with adjusted [genre/tone settings](/docs/legacy-wiki/ai-wizard "AI Wizard").
- **Overwritten edits** → Lock approved lines in the [Script Editor](/docs/legacy-wiki/script-editor "Script Editor").

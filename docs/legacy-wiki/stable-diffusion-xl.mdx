## Overview

The **Stable Diffusion XL (SDXL) Integration** in [ACT 3 AI](/docs/legacy-wiki/act3ai "ACT3AI") enables creators to generate high-quality, photorealistic or stylized still images that can be used as [storyboard panels](/docs/legacy-wiki/storyboard-panels "Storyboard Panels"), [set backgrounds](/docs/legacy-wiki/set-design "Set Design"), concept art, or even as textures in [Blender projects](/docs/legacy-wiki/blender-integration "Blender Integration").
This integration is ideal for detailed previsualization and creative experimentation before committing to full video rendering.

## Key Capabilities

- **High-Resolution Image Generation:** Produce images up to 2048×2048 pixels for detailed visual concepts.
- **Prompt-Based Styling:** Describe the desired look, mood, or subject, and SDXL will generate matching visuals.
- **Style Consistency:** Apply custom models or LoRA files to maintain a consistent aesthetic across assets.
- **Image-to-Image Mode:** Transform existing images into new variations while preserving composition.
- **Batch Output:** Generate multiple variations from a single prompt for faster selection.

## How to Use

1. Open [Editor](/docs/legacy-wiki/editor "Editor") or [Storyboard Panels](/docs/legacy-wiki/storyboard-panels "Storyboard Panels") view.
2. Select **AI → Stable Diffusion XL** from the AI tools menu.
3. Enter your prompt, including style, setting, lighting, and subject details.
4. Optionally upload a base image for Image-to-Image transformation.
5. Adjust resolution, seed, and model settings as needed.
6. Click **Generate** and review results in your asset library.

## Pricing & Credits

- SDXL image generation uses [credits](/docs/legacy-wiki/credit-system "Credit System") based on resolution and number of variations.
- Example: 1024×1024 image = 0.5 credits; 2048×2048 image = 1 credit.
- Image-to-Image mode costs the same as text-to-image generation.

## Tips & Best Practices

- Be descriptive—include camera style, lighting, colors, and mood in prompts for best results.
- Use consistent keywords across multiple prompts to keep your project visually cohesive.
- For scene matching, start with an Image-to-Image workflow using reference frames from your [shots](/docs/legacy-wiki/shots "Shots").
- Save your favorite prompts for reuse in future projects.

## Common Use Cases

- Generating [storyboards](/docs/legacy-wiki/storyboard-panels "Storyboard Panels") quickly before animating.
- Creating [set designs](/docs/legacy-wiki/set-design "Set Design") for fantasy, sci-fi, or period pieces.
- Producing concept art for [character](/docs/legacy-wiki/characters "Characters") outfits and props.
- Making textures and backgrounds for [Blender scenes](/docs/legacy-wiki/blender-integration "Blender Integration").

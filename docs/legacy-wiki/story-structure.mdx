## Overview

The **Story Structure** feature in [ACT 3 AI](/docs/legacy-wiki/act3ai "ACT3AI") organizes your project into a narrative hierarchy of **Acts → Scenes → Shots**, with optional **[Story Beats](/docs/legacy-wiki/story-beats "Story Beats")** in between.
This structure helps ensure your story flows logically, maintains thematic consistency, and is easy to manage through [AI Wizard](/docs/legacy-wiki/ai-wizard "AI Wizard"), [Editor](/docs/legacy-wiki/editor "Editor"), and [Storyboard Panels](/docs/legacy-wiki/storyboard-panels "Storyboard Panels").

## Why It Matters

- **Creative Clarity:** Keeps writers, directors, and editors aligned.
- **Pacing Control:** Balances action, tension, and resolution.
- **Production Planning:** Converts abstract ideas into concrete [scenes](/docs/legacy-wiki/scenes "Scenes") and [shots](/docs/legacy-wiki/shots "Shots") for scheduling and rendering.
- **Collaboration:** Allows multiple contributors to work on the same structure in real time.

## Core Levels

- **Acts** – Major sections of your story (e.g., Setup, Conflict, Resolution).
- **Story Beats** – Key narrative moments within an act. See [Story Beats](/docs/legacy-wiki/story-beats "Story Beats").
- **Scenes** – Specific time/place interactions with character goals. See [Scenes](/docs/legacy-wiki/scenes "Scenes").
- **Shots** – Individual camera views or moments. See [Shots](/docs/legacy-wiki/shots "Shots").

## Workflow in ACT 3 AI

1. Start a [New Project](/docs/legacy-wiki/new-project "New Project") in [AI Wizard](/docs/legacy-wiki/ai-wizard "AI Wizard") and set genre, tone, and audience.
2. Generate a suggested act and beat structure.
3. Refine acts in the [Editor](/docs/legacy-wiki/editor "Editor").
4. Expand beats into [scenes](/docs/legacy-wiki/scenes "Scenes").
5. Break scenes into [shots](/docs/legacy-wiki/shots "Shots") using [Storyboard Panels](/docs/legacy-wiki/storyboard-panels "Storyboard Panels") and [Top Down View](/docs/legacy-wiki/top-down-view "Top Down View").
6. Preview renders in [Render Queue](/docs/legacy-wiki/render-queue "Render Queue") and assemble the final output in [Build Video](/docs/legacy-wiki/build-video "Build Video").

## Tips for Effective Story Structure

- Keep each beat focused on one clear action or emotional turn.
- Alternate high-intensity and low-intensity beats for pacing.
- Ensure every scene moves the story forward or deepens character.
- Tag scenes with thematic or plot labels to track threads.

## Overview

**Camera Movement** in [ACT 3 AI](/docs/legacy-wiki/act3ai "ACT3AI") refers to how the virtual camera travels through a scene during a [shot](/docs/legacy-wiki/shots "Shots").
By defining movement, you can control pacing, emotion, and perspective, just like in traditional cinematography.
These instructions are entered as part of [camera instructions](/docs/legacy-wiki/camera-instructions "Camera Instructions") in the [Editor workspace](/docs/legacy-wiki/editor "Editor") or [shot prompts](/docs/legacy-wiki/shot-prompting "Shot Prompting").

## Why It Matters

- Establishes mood (e.g., slow dolly = dramatic tension, shaky cam = chaos).
- Guides audience focus to key elements.
- Creates cinematic flow between [scenes](/docs/legacy-wiki/scenes "Scenes") and [shots](/docs/legacy-wiki/shots "Shots").
- Matches genre conventions (handheld for horror, Steadicam for action, aerials for epics).

## Supported Camera Movements

- **Pan** – Rotate left/right from a fixed point.
- **Tilt** – Move camera angle up/down while staying in place.
- **Dolly** – Move camera closer/further (in/out) along rails.
- **Truck (Tracking)** – Move camera sideways (left/right).
- **Crane / Jib** – Lift camera up/down smoothly.
- **Handheld** – Shaky movement simulating handheld rigs.
- **Steadicam** – Smooth movement following a subject.
- **Zoom** – Change focal length (not physical movement, but often combined).
- **Rack Focus with Movement** – Shift focus while moving toward/away from subject.
- **Drone / Aerial** – Simulate overhead or sweeping drone shots.

## How to Use in ACT 3 AI

1. Open your [Editor](/docs/legacy-wiki/editor "Editor") or [Storyboard](/docs/legacy-wiki/storyboard-panels "Storyboard Panels").
2. Select a [shot](/docs/legacy-wiki/shots "Shots") and enter movement into the prompt (e.g., “dolly in on hero’s face as music swells”).
3. Choose rendering engine ([Google Veo 3](/docs/legacy-wiki/google-veo-3 "Google Veo 3"), [Runway Integration](/docs/legacy-wiki/runway "Runway"), [WAN AI Integration](/docs/legacy-wiki/wan "Wan")).
4. Preview the movement in [Timeline](/docs/legacy-wiki/timeline "Timeline") or generate a draft preview.
5. Adjust prompt wording until you achieve the desired look.

## Example Prompts

- “Tracking shot following the character down a neon-lit alley, Steadicam smoothness.”
- “Slow dolly in toward the table as the detective reveals the evidence.”
- “Handheld close-up of astronaut breathing heavily, shaky and tense.”
- “Drone aerial sweeping across desert landscape at sunset.”

## Integration with Workflow

- [Script Editor](/docs/legacy-wiki/script-editor "Script Editor") – Add movement notes in parentheticals.
- [Top-Down View](/docs/legacy-wiki/top-down-view "Top Down View") – Place cameras and draw paths to simulate dolly/tracking shots.
- [Storyboards & Panels](/docs/legacy-wiki/storyboard-panels "Storyboard Panels") – Visualize movement frame by frame.
- [Build Video](/docs/legacy-wiki/build-video "Build Video") – Finalize sequence flow with multiple moving shots.

## Best Practices

- Keep movement instructions short and cinematic (10–15 words).
- Match movement to emotion — fast pans for urgency, slow dollies for intimacy.
- Break complex movements into multiple [shots](/docs/legacy-wiki/shots "Shots") rather than one overloaded prompt.
- Use [timeline scrub](/docs/legacy-wiki/timeline "Timeline") to refine pacing between movements.

## Troubleshooting

- **Movement feels unnatural** → Try simpler instructions or break into two shots.
- **AI ignores camera direction** → Use standard cinematography terms (pan, tilt, dolly).
- **Too much shake in handheld** → Specify “light handheld” vs “intense shaky cam.”

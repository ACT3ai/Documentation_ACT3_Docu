## Overview

The **Character Editor** in [ACT 3 AI](/docs/legacy-wiki/act3ai "ACT3AI") is the workspace where you create, customize, and manage [characters](/docs/legacy-wiki/characters "Characters") for your projects.
It allows writers and directors to define traits, personalities, and visual attributes, ensuring that characters remain consistent across [scenes](/docs/legacy-wiki/scenes "Scenes"), [shots](/docs/legacy-wiki/shots "Shots"), and entire productions.

The Character Editor bridges the gap between the creative side of [AI Writing](/docs/legacy-wiki/ai-writing "AI Writing") and the performance layer of [Digital Actors](/docs/legacy-wiki/digital-actors "Digital Actors").

## Key Capabilities

- **Profile Creation:** Define character names, roles, and narrative importance.
- **Traits & Attributes:** Add details such as age, gender, ethnicity, occupation, and backstory.
- **Personality & Motivation:** Specify emotional tone, core drives, and dramatic arcs.
- **Appearance Preview:** Visualize initial looks before assigning a [Digital Actor](/docs/legacy-wiki/digital-actors "Digital Actors").
- **Voice & Style:** Link voice presets or AI dubbing for dialogue consistency.
- **Reuse & Sharing:** Store characters in the [Actor Library](/docs/legacy-wiki/actor-library-2 "Actor Library") for use across projects.

## How to Use

1. Open a [New Project](/docs/legacy-wiki/new-project "New Project") or an existing one.
2. Navigate to the **Character Editor** from the sidebar or script view.
3. Click **New Character** to create a profile.
4. Add traits (name, role, age, personality, backstory).
5. Optionally assign a [Digital Actor](/docs/legacy-wiki/digital-actors "Digital Actors") for visualization.
6. Save to the [Actor Library](/docs/legacy-wiki/actor-library-2 "Actor Library") for reuse.

## Integration with Workflow

- [AI Wizard](/docs/legacy-wiki/ai-wizard "AI Wizard") → Suggests characters during story generation.
- [Script Editor](/docs/legacy-wiki/script-editor "Script Editor") → Characters appear in dialogue formatting automatically.
- [Cast](/docs/legacy-wiki/cast "Cast") → Characters are mapped to [Digital Actors](/docs/legacy-wiki/digital-actors "Digital Actors").
- [Storyboards & Panels](/docs/legacy-wiki/storyboard-panels "Storyboard Panels") → Characters populate panels visually.
- [Build Video](/docs/legacy-wiki/build-video "Build Video") → Ensures continuity of character presence throughout renders.

## Best Practices

- Define character motivations early to guide story beats.
- Use consistent naming to avoid mismatches in script formatting.
- Keep multiple versions (young/older, alternate wardrobe) saved under one profile.
- Assign placeholder actors until casting is finalized.

## Troubleshooting

- **Character not showing in script editor** → Check name formatting in profile.
- **Missing from Actor Library** → Save character after editing.
- **Wrong actor linked** → Reassign via the Cast Manager.

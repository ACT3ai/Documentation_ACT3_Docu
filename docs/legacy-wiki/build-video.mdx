## Overview

The **Build Video** feature in [ACT 3 AI](/docs/legacy-wiki/act3ai "ACT3AI") is the final assembly stage where your [scenes](/docs/legacy-wiki/scenes "Scenes"), [shots](/docs/legacy-wiki/shots "Shots"), and [B-Roll](/docs/legacy-wiki/b-roll "B-roll") come together into a finished production.
It provides an intuitive interface for arranging clips, applying transitions, adding audio, and exporting your project in professional formats.

Whether your content was generated by AI, imported from external sources, or a combination of both, the Build Video process ensures it is polished, synced, and ready for publication.

## Key Capabilities

- **Multi-Track Timeline:** Combine [AI-generated video](/docs/legacy-wiki/ai-rendered-footage "AI Rendered Footage"), [B-Roll](/docs/legacy-wiki/b-roll "B-roll"), [music](/docs/legacy-wiki/audio-tracks "Audio Tracks"), and sound effects on separate layers.
- **Transitions & Effects:** Apply crossfades, wipes, overlays, and filters between [shots](/docs/legacy-wiki/shots "Shots").
- **Audio Mixing:** Adjust dialogue, music, and effects levels independently.
- **Aspect Ratio Control:** Export in multiple formats (16:9, 9:16, 1:1).
- **Render Queue Integration:** Send final compositions to the [Render Queue](/docs/legacy-wiki/render-queue "Render Queue") for processing.
- **Preview & Adjust:** Real-time preview with frame-by-frame accuracy before rendering.

## How to Build Your Video

1. Open your project in the [Editor](/docs/legacy-wiki/editor "Editor") and navigate to the **Build Video** mode.
2. Drag and drop [scenes](/docs/legacy-wiki/scenes "Scenes"), [shots](/docs/legacy-wiki/shots "Shots"), and [B-Roll](/docs/legacy-wiki/b-roll "B-roll") into the [timeline](/docs/legacy-wiki/timeline "Timeline").
3. Arrange the sequence in your desired order.
4. Add transitions between shots if needed.
5. Layer in [background music](/docs/legacy-wiki/audio-tracks "Audio Tracks") and effects.
6. Use color grading to ensure visual consistency.
7. Preview the entire video and make final adjustments.

## Export Options

- Formats: MP4, MOV, ProRes
- Resolutions: 720p, 1080p, 4K
- Frame Rates: 24fps, 30fps, 60fps

## Best Practices

- Keep pacing consistent with your [story arc](/docs/legacy-wiki/story-arcs "Story Arcs") and tone.
- Match lighting and color grading across all clips.
- Test exports in lower resolution before rendering in full 4K to save [credits](/docs/legacy-wiki/credit-system "Credit System").
- Use scene locking to prevent last-minute accidental changes.

## Credits & Billing

- Rendering consumes [credits](/docs/legacy-wiki/credit-system "Credit System") based on resolution, length, and chosen rendering engine ([Google Veo 3](/docs/legacy-wiki/google-veo-3 "Google Veo 3"), [WAN AI](/docs/legacy-wiki/wan "Wan"), [Runway Integration](/docs/legacy-wiki/runway "Runway")).
- Check the [Credits Panel](/docs/legacy-wiki/credit-system "Credit System") for real-time usage before exporting.
- Exporting at lower resolutions or shorter lengths reduces credit consumption.

## Troubleshooting

- **Video looks different after export** → Ensure color grading is applied before rendering.
- **Audio out of sync** → Check frame rate settings and adjust before final render.
- **Long render times** → Queue overnight using the [Render Queue](/docs/legacy-wiki/render-queue "Render Queue").

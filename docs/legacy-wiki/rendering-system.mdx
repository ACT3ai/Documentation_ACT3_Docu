## Overview

The **Rendering System** in [ACT 3 AI](/docs/legacy-wiki/act3ai "ACT3AI") powers the transformation of [scripts](/docs/legacy-wiki/scripts "Scripts"), [scenes](/docs/legacy-wiki/scenes "Scenes"), and [digital actors](/docs/legacy-wiki/digital-actors "Digital Actors") into AI-generated video.
It connects creative inputs (scripts, storyboards, timelines) with compute-intensive AI models like [Google\_Veo\_3](/docs/legacy-wiki/google-veo-3 "Google Veo 3"), [Runway](/docs/legacy-wiki/runway "Runway"), and [WAN AI](/docs/legacy-wiki/wan "Wan"), delivering both quick previews and production-quality outputs.

## Key Components

- **Rendering Engines:** Specialized AI models for different visual styles.
- **Resolution & Quality Settings:** Draft, Medium, High, Ultra/4K.
- **Credit Usage:** Each render consumes [credits](/docs/legacy-wiki/credit-system "Credit System") depending on resolution, duration, and model used.
- **[Render Queue](/docs/legacy-wiki/render-queue "Render Queue"):** Manages all rendering jobs.
- **Preview vs Final Renders:** Quick low-res iterations before committing to final exports.

## Supported Engines

- [Google Veo 3 Integration](/docs/legacy-wiki/google-veo-3 "Google Veo 3") – photorealistic cinematic output.
- [Runway Integration](/docs/legacy-wiki/runway "Runway") – stylized and experimental visuals.
- [WAN AI Integration](/docs/legacy-wiki/wan "Wan") – low-latency collaborative previews.
- [Stable Diffusion XL / Flux](/docs/legacy-wiki/stable-diffusion-xl "Stable Diffusion XL") – creative generation (experimental).
- [Comfy UI Integration](/docs/legacy-wiki/comfy-ui "Comfy UI") – advanced node-based rendering for technical users.

## Render Workflow

1. Prepare [scenes](/docs/legacy-wiki/scenes "Scenes") and [shots](/docs/legacy-wiki/shots "Shots") in the [Editor](/docs/legacy-wiki/editor "Editor").
2. Select **Render → Add to [Queue](/docs/legacy-wiki/render-queue "Render Queue")**.
3. Choose settings:
   1. Resolution (Draft, 720p, 1080p, 4K).
   2. Quality (low-cost draft → cinematic).
   3. Engine (Google Veo 3, Runway, WAN AI, etc.).
4. Confirm estimated [credit cost](/docs/legacy-wiki/credit-system "Credit System").
5. Render job runs in the [Render Queue](/docs/legacy-wiki/render-queue "Render Queue") until complete.
6. Download output or send to [Build Video](/docs/legacy-wiki/build-video "Build Video") / [Export Distribution](/docs/legacy-wiki/export-distribution "Export Distribution").

## Resolution & Credits

| Resolution | Preview (per 10s) | Final Render (per 10s) |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Draft (low-res) | 1 credit | N/A |
| 720p | 2 credits | 4 credits |
| 1080p | 2 credits | 5 credits |
| 4K | 4 credits | 10 credits |

## Preview vs Final Rendering

- **Previews:** Fast, low-cost, lower resolution, used for timing and rough visualization.
- **Final Renders:** High resolution, polished visuals, suitable for distribution.
- Strategy: Use draft previews frequently; only commit to final renders once locked.

## Best Practices

- Always generate draft previews before 4K outputs to save credits.
- Use [WAN AI](/docs/legacy-wiki/wan "Wan") sessions for brainstorming and collaborative testing.
- Group jobs into the [Render Queue](/docs/legacy-wiki/render-queue "Render Queue") overnight to avoid bottlenecks.
- Keep track of [credit usage](/docs/legacy-wiki/credit-system "Credit System") in the dashboard.

## Troubleshooting

- **Job not rendering** → Check [queue status](/docs/legacy-wiki/render-queue "Render Queue") and [credit balance](/docs/legacy-wiki/credit-system "Credit System").
- **Output looks inconsistent** → Verify prompts and locked [character profiles](/docs/legacy-wiki/characters "Characters").
- **Slow rendering** → Reduce resolution or batch at off-peak times.
- **Missing assets** → Ensure [B-Roll](/docs/legacy-wiki/import-broll "Import BRoll") or [audio files](/docs/legacy-wiki/audio-tracks "Audio Tracks") are attached.

## Serious production at real runtime

The defining characteristic of enterprise film work is duration. Everything gets harder past twenty minutes: continuity, review, coordination, cost.

ACT 3 addresses runtime structurally. Projects use a Show ▸ Season ▸ Episode ▸ Scene ▸ Shot hierarchy with traceable assets and linked metadata. Target duration drives pacing — the beat → scene → shot planner computes shot lists and embeds cinematography metadata per shot. Generation runs in bulk with background job queueing on dedicated GPU nodes. Approved shots stitch into scenes and episodes automatically. And critically, you review the **entire** 1-hour show or 2-hour movie on a unified Adobe Premiere timeline, zooming from the full runtime to single-frame detail before anything ships.

The working model matches production reality too: 2–3 hour sprints in a purpose-built editor with a three-column swappable layout, zoomable timeline, smooth scrubbing, selection-based playback, keyboard navigation, and tagging for bulk operations across hundreds of shots.

## Teams working in one production

Enterprise productions have writers, directors, cinematographers, VFX artists, editors, producers, and legal reviewers — often across companies.

**Permissions** are granular by design:

- **Read** — view projects and scripts, no changes
- **Modify/Edit** — edit scripts, characters, scenes; no AI generation
- **Run AI** — initiate AI tasks (implies Modify/Edit)
- **Use Credits** — initiate credit-consuming tasks; the primary resource control
- **Billing** — manage subscription, payment, invoices; no member changes
- **Owner** — full control including member management and ownership transfer

**Collaboration** is real-time and version-controlled with full change history and concurrent work without conflicts. Persona-aware layouts give writers, directors, and actors role-appropriate views. Comment threads sit on character profiles, co-editing shows presence cursors, and lock-down controls freeze approved pages, scenes, and shots as read-only so nothing signed off gets overwritten. Membership is managed by invitation with permissions assigned at invite time, and removal revokes access immediately.

**Organizations** are the unit of tenancy and billing — an isolated workspace owning projects, members, subscription plan, credit pool, and payment method, with a persistent switcher for people who work across multiple companies.

## Ownership of intellectual property

For an enterprise, this is often the deciding criterion.

- The **Organization legally owns** all projects, created content, and generated assets, stated in the Terms of Service.
- **Ownership transfer** is possible but requires confirmation from both parties, preventing unauthorized moves.
- **Private assets**: sets and characters can be kept private within your organization rather than exposed to any community library. Enterprise plans include private 3D sets.
- **Everything in one place**: scripts, story arcs, characters, digital actors, wardrobe, sets, prompts, renders, and masters live in the production, not scattered across individual users' tool accounts.
- **Commercial-use rights** scale with plan tier; Business and above include commercial use.
- **Content safety** is enforced at three stages — prompts before generation, scripts before production, and finished outputs before download — with auto-redaction or admin review.

That combination is what "we store all the intellectual property" means in practice: your company's production assets consolidate into a workspace you control, with per-member permissions and an audit trail.

## Cost governance

Enterprise buyers need spend to be predictable and attributable:

- Credits pool at the **Organization** level, not per user.
- The **Use Credits** permission gates who can spend at all.
- Every action shows its **exact credit cost before commit**, including multiple quality tiers (e.g. Q=1/Q=2/Q=3 at different costs).
- The **render queue** shows predicted GPU-minute spend so producers approve or postpone per job.
- A **billing dashboard** meters video minutes, storage, and concurrent renders with real-time spend forecasts and budget alerts.
- **Progress panels** show live logs and percentage complete, with cancel to reclaim credits instantly.
- Unused credits move to a **rollover bank** up to a per-plan cap.

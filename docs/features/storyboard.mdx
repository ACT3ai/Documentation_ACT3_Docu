# Storyboard

The Storyboard feature lets you visually map out your shots before or during production. Each storyboard panel is linked directly to a shot in your script and timeline, giving you a frame-by-frame preview of your visual story before any video is rendered.

## What Storyboards Do

Storyboards serve as the visual blueprint for your production:

- Visualize shot composition and framing before generating video
- Communicate camera angles and action to your team
- Catch continuity errors before they become expensive render problems
- Generate client-ready previsualization materials early in the process
- Link script text, camera instructions, and visual panels in one place

## Creating Storyboard Panels

Open the Storyboard Pane from the editor layout and select a scene or shot to begin.

**Three ways to create panels:**

**1. AI Generation** — Click **+ Add Panel → AI Generate**. Enter a visual prompt describing the shot composition, lighting, and subject. Choose a rendering engine (Google Veo 3 for realistic previews, Runway for stylized, Wan 2.1 for fast low-cost panels). The AI generates a still image panel representing the shot.

**2. Manual Upload** — Upload your own sketches, reference images, or drawn storyboard frames in PNG, JPG, or SVG format.

**3. Auto-Generate from Script** — Let ACT3 AI interpret your shot descriptions and scene headings to generate panels automatically. Click **Auto-Generate Panels** on a scene to generate all shots at once.

## Panel Structure

Each storyboard panel contains:

- **Visual frame** — The image representing the shot
- **Shot name and number** — Linked to the corresponding shot in the script and timeline
- **Camera notes** — Angle, movement, and lens information
- **Scene context** — Location, time of day, and characters present
- **Action description** — Brief text describing what happens in this shot

## Editing Panels

- Rename panels for clarity (e.g., "CU — Hero's Eyes" or "Wide — Establishing City")
- Add or update camera movement and lighting notes
- Attach reference images for production planning
- Reorder panels using drag and drop
- Delete or replace panels without affecting the underlying script

## AI-Generated Panels

Credit usage for AI panels:

- Google Veo 3 panels — Photographic realism, standard credit rate
- Runway panels — Stylized output, standard credit rate
- Wan 2.1 panels — Fast, low-cost, lower visual fidelity

Panels are static images (not video). Credit consumption is much lower than full video renders.

## Storyboard and Timeline Integration

Panels link bidirectionally with the timeline:

- Selecting a panel in the Storyboard Pane highlights the corresponding shot in the timeline
- Reordering shots in the timeline updates panel sequence automatically
- Script text linked to each panel shows in the side-by-side view
- Timeline scrubbing updates the active panel in the Storyboard Pane

## Multi-View Layout

Work with multiple views open simultaneously:

- Script text on the left, storyboard panels in the center, timeline below
- See scene description, camera notes, and visual panel in one glance
- Switch between panel grid view (overview) and panel detail view (single shot focus)

## Exporting Storyboards

Export your storyboard for client review, pitch decks, or team reference:

- **PDF** — Print-ready, client-facing format with all panel images and notes
- **PNG/JPG sheets** — Individual panel images for quick sharing
- **Linked media** — Embed panels in documentation or presentation decks

Exports maintain script-to-panel links for easy reference in the exported document.

## Collaboration on Storyboards

- Team members can co-edit storyboard panels in real time
- Leave comments on individual panels for shot-level feedback
- Lock panels once approved to prevent changes
- Version history tracks panel additions and modifications

## Best Practices

- Generate quick Wan 2.1 panels first to establish visual flow cheaply
- Replace key panels with higher-quality Google Veo 3 or Runway images for client presentations
- Use panels as a communication tool between writers and directors before expensive rendering
- Keep panels clear and focused on one action or camera moment per frame
- Lock panels once approved to signal their status to the production team

## Troubleshooting

**Panel not linked to its shot** — Drag the panel thumbnail onto the correct shot in the outline to re-link it.

**AI generation result is off-target** — Refine your visual prompt or switch to a different rendering engine.

**Missing panels in export** — Ensure all panels are saved before exporting. Unsaved panels may not appear.

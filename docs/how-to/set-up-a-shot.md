# How to Set Up a Shot

**Goal:** Define a specific shot so that when the AI renders it, you get the image you had in your head — the right angle, the right framing, the right action.

Every rendered clip in ACT3 AI starts as a shot definition. The more clearly you define the shot, the more accurately the AI renders it.

---

## Steps

1. **Navigate to the scene.** In the left navigation, go to the scene where this shot belongs.

2. **Add a new shot.** In the scene's shot list, click **+ Add Shot**. A new shot panel opens.

3. **Set the shot type.** In the Camera tab, choose from 22 standard shot types — Wide Shot, Medium Shot, Close-Up, Over the Shoulder, POV, Bird's Eye View, Dutch Angle, and more. Pick the one that serves the story moment.

4. **Set the framing subject.** Specify who or what is being framed — a character name, "the door," "the crowd." This tells the AI where to focus the camera.

5. **Write the action description.** In the action field, describe what happens in the shot. Be specific: *"Marcus turns to face the window, his reflection visible in the glass."* Vague descriptions produce vague results.

6. **Set camera movement.** Go to the Motion tab. Choose the camera move — Static, Pan, Tilt, Dolly, Track, Crane, Handheld — or leave it static if nothing moves.

7. **Check the lighting.** The shot inherits the scene's lighting setup. If this shot needs different lighting, override it in the Lighting tab.

8. **Review the assembled AI prompt.** The Prompt tab shows you the full text the AI will use to generate this shot. You can read it, edit specific parts, or leave it as generated.

9. **Generate the shot.** Click **Generate** and choose a quality level — Draft (fast, lower quality) or Standard/High (slower, better). The shot renders and appears in the timeline.

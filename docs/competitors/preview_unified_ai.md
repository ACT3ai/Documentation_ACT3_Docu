## Why clip-by-clip review misleads you

A clip in isolation is judged on its own merits: is the framing good, is the character right, is the motion clean. A clip *in a cut* is judged on completely different criteria, and they are the ones that decide whether the video works:

- **Pacing.** Does this shot hold too long after the one before it? You cannot know without the one before it.
- **Rhythm across a sequence.** Three medium shots in a row read as flat. Individually, each was fine.
- **Continuity drift.** Wardrobe, lighting direction and set dressing errors are invisible per shot and glaring in sequence.
- **Tonal consistency.** A comedic beat landing inside a tense sequence only reveals itself in context.
- **Total runtime and structure.** Act breaks, midpoint, the shape of the whole thing — only visible at full length.

Every one of those failures is cheap to fix early and expensive to fix late. Clip-by-clip review systematically hides them until late.

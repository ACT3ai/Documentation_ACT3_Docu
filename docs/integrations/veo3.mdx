# Google Veo 3.1

Google Veo 3.1 is ACT3 AI's primary high-quality video generation engine. It produces photorealistic, cinematic output from text prompts and is the best choice for final-quality renders where visual fidelity matters most. See [cinematography](/filmmakers/cinematography) for how to write effective camera instructions in your prompts.

## What Google Veo 3.1 Does

Veo 3.1 converts your [shot](/concepts/shots) prompts into short AI-generated video clips. Submit a detailed description of action, setting, and style, and Veo 3.1 returns a rendered clip in your Preview pane. Accepted clips go directly onto your timeline or into the [Render Queue](/features/render-queue) for final export.

## Key Capabilities

- **Prompt-to-Video** — Text descriptions become cinematic video clips
- **Photorealistic Output** — Film-quality visuals with natural lighting and motion
- **Resolution and Frame Rate** — Up to 1080p at 30fps
- **Style Presets** — Apply Google's built-in artistic filters or custom style parameters
- **Batch Requests** — Queue multiple prompts for parallel generation and monitor in the Render Queue
- **Camera Instructions** — Responds accurately to cinematography language: "dolly in," "tracking shot," "handheld," etc.

## How to Use

1. In the Editor, select a shot or scene and click **AI → Google Veo 3.1**
2. Your shot prompt is pre-populated from the shot description — edit as needed
3. Include action, camera movement, lighting, and mood in the prompt
4. Choose output settings: resolution and frame rate
5. Click **Generate** — the request goes to Google Veo 3.1 and the clip appears in the Preview pane
6. Accept the clip, refine the prompt and [regenerate](/how-to/regenerate-a-shot), or swap to a different engine

## Prompt Tips for Veo 3.1

Veo 3.1 performs best with clear, specific, cinematic prompts:

- Keep prompts under 100 words
- Use standard film terminology (dolly, pan, tracking, close-up, establishing shot)
- Describe lighting explicitly (golden hour, film noir, studio lighting, overcast day)
- Include mood and atmosphere words (tense, serene, chaotic, mysterious)
- For longer takes, split into consecutive prompts and stitch in the Timeline

**Example prompts:**

- *"Medium shot of detective at a rain-streaked window, handheld camera, warm interior light vs cold blue exterior, reflections in glass, tense atmosphere."*
- *"Wide establishing shot of Mars colony at sunrise, drone aerial, red dust plains, domed habitat, epic and lonely."*
- *"Slow dolly in on two characters at a dinner table, candlelight, soft bokeh background, intimate and strained."*

## Credit Usage

Google Veo 3.1 generation is billed per second of output:

- Approximately 1 credit per second at 1080p
- Draft-quality renders cost less
- Actual rates are shown in the credit estimate before you confirm generation

Check your credit balance in the [Credits](/account/credits) Panel before queuing large batches.

## Best Use Cases

- Final-quality video renders for distribution
- Cinematic film and narrative content
- Photorealistic environments and character scenes
- Any content where visual realism is the priority

## When to Use Other Engines

- Use **Wan 2.1** for fast creative iteration and team collaboration previews
- Use **Grok Video** for creative or stylistically distinctive output where photorealism is secondary

## Troubleshooting

**Results don't match the prompt** — Break the prompt into shorter, more specific phrases. Avoid conflicting directions in a single prompt.

**Generation takes too long** — Large batch requests may queue. Use Wan 2.1 for rapid iteration and switch to Veo 3.1 for final approved shots.

**Credits running out** — Use draft previews during the creative phase. Only commit Veo 3.1 renders to approved shots.

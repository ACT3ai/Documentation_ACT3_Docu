# Shots

A **Shot** is a single continuous camera angle within a [Beat](/docs/concepts/beats).
Shots are the atomic unit of video generation in ACT3 AI — each shot produces one
video clip rendered by the AI model of your choice.

## Shot Types

ACT3 AI supports all standard cinematic shot types:

| Shot Type | Abbreviation | Description |
|-----------|-------------|-------------|
| Extreme Wide Shot | EWS | Establishes location; subject tiny in frame |
| Wide Shot | WS | Full subject visible; environment prominent |
| Medium Shot | MS | Subject from waist up |
| Close-Up | CU | Face or object fills frame |
| Extreme Close-Up | ECU | Detail shot — eyes, hands, props |
| Over the Shoulder | OTS | Camera behind one character, facing another |
| Point of View | POV | Camera sees what the character sees |
| Two Shot | 2S | Two characters in frame together |

## Camera Movement

Each shot can include one camera movement:

| Movement | Effect |
|----------|--------|
| **Static** | Camera holds still |
| **Pan** | Camera rotates left or right on its axis |
| **Tilt** | Camera rotates up or down on its axis |
| **Dolly** | Camera physically moves forward or backward |
| **Tracking** | Camera moves parallel to a moving subject |
| **Crane / Jib** | Camera rises or descends vertically |
| **Handheld** | Slight natural movement for documentary feel |
| **Drone** | Aerial perspective with forward drift |

## Shot Settings in ACT3 AI

When you create or edit a shot, you configure:

- **Shot type** — from the list above
- **Camera movement** — from the list above
- **Lens / focal length** — wide (24mm), normal (50mm), telephoto (85mm+)
- **Lighting preset** — golden hour, overcast, studio, night, etc.
- **Subject description** — what (or who) is in frame and what they're doing
- **Background / set** — location or Blender scene to use
- **Duration** — target clip length in seconds (2–10 sec recommended for AI gen)
- **AI model** — Veo 3, Runway, ComfyUI, Hunyuan, or Wan 2.1

## Generating Video from a Shot

1. Open a Beat in the editor
2. Click **+ Add Shot**
3. Fill in shot type, movement, subject, and duration
4. Click **Generate**
5. ACT3 AI sends the prompt to the selected AI model
6. The rendered clip appears in the shot card for review

You can regenerate any shot with different settings or swap to a different AI model
without losing your other shots.

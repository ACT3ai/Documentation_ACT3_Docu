# Acts

An **Act** is a major narrative division of your project. Acts are the highest level of story structure — they contain [scenes](/concepts/scenes) and [shots](/concepts/shots), and they define the pacing, tone, and dramatic progression of your video or film.

The ACT3 AI platform uses a standard hierarchy: **Act → Scene → [Beat](/concepts/beats) → Shot → Output**

## The Three-Act Structure

Most films and video projects use a three-act structure. ACT3 AI defaults to this format, though you can modify the number of acts for any project:

**Act 1 — Setup**
Introduces the world, characters, and central conflict. Ends with the inciting incident or "point of no return" that propels the story into Act 2.

**Act 2 — Confrontation**
Expands the conflict, develops character relationships, and raises the stakes. This is typically the longest act. Ends with a major low point or complication that forces the climax.

**Act 3 — Resolution**
Delivers the climax and resolves the central conflict. Brings character arcs to completion and leaves the audience with a clear emotional outcome.

## Flexible Act Structures

ACT3 AI is not limited to three acts. You can configure any number of acts to match your format:

- **Four-act structure** — common for TV drama, with a stronger midpoint break
- **Five-act structure** — classical theatrical structure
- **Single act** — for short-form social content under 60 seconds
- **Multi-episode** — each episode treated as a separate project or as acts within a larger series

## Using Acts in ACT3 AI

1. Start a new project or open an existing one
2. Use the [AI Wizard](/how-to/build-story-structure) to automatically generate acts from your logline or concept
3. Add or edit acts manually in the [Script Editor](/features/script-editing) or Timeline
4. Organize scenes within each act for structure and pacing
5. Lock acts once finalized to prevent major changes during production

## Acts in the Timeline

Acts appear as labeled sections in the Timeline editor, making it easy to navigate large projects. You can:

- Jump directly to any act from the timeline overview
- Filter the Top-Down View by act
- Set pacing and tone properties per act that influence AI generation
- Use Build Video to maintain act divisions when reviewing or exporting

## Act Properties

| Property | Description |
|---|---|
| **Name** | Label used in the editor (e.g., "Act 1 — Setup") |
| **Theme** | The dominant dramatic purpose or emotional arc |
| **Tone** | Visual and atmospheric style for AI generation |
| **Target Duration** | Planned screen time |
| **Status** | Draft, In Progress, or Locked |

## AI Wizard and Acts

When you run the AI Wizard with a logline, it generates act outlines that include:

- A description of the act's narrative purpose
- Suggested scenes with locations and characters
- Key story beats within each act
- Pacing notes and dramatic turning points

You can regenerate any act independently without affecting others.

## Best Practices

- Define acts early for clarity before adding scene-level details
- Keep each act focused on a single narrative purpose
- Use story beats to break acts into smaller units for pacing control — see [Beats](/concepts/beats) for how to work with them
- For fast-paced content like social media ads, shorten Act 1 significantly and extend Act 2

## Troubleshooting

**Too few acts** — Add additional acts if your story feels rushed or underdeveloped at the scene level.

**Too many acts** — Consider merging acts if your narrative pacing slows or becomes confusing for the audience.

**AI-generated acts do not match your vision** — Edit manually in the [Script Editor](/features/script-editing). The AI Wizard is a starting point, not a final decision.
